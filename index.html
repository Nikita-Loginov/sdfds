Choose your
soulmate type of yoga

hatha

The main elements are certain static poses (asanas), breathing exercises (pranayama) and relaxation.The goal of Hatha yoga is to achieve peace of mind and balance between body, spirit and the surrounding world.

vinyasa

Vinyasa yoga resembles a smooth dance in which your body and breath merge together.Dynamic performance of asanas will increase muscle elasticity, cleanse the body of toxins and improve blood circulation.

fly yoga

Yoga classes are held in special hammocks attached to the ceiling or horizontal bar.Yoga in hammocks gives additional opportunities to perform asanas easier, such as deflections, extensions, somersaults and inverted positions.

ashtanga

Ashtanga yoga is a popular teaching in India, in the West, in Russia, a kind of Hatha yoga, which is characterized by high dynamics, requires good physical fitness and maximum calculation. It is quite possible to replace fitness with Ashtanga yoga.

bikram

Bikram Yoga is a hot yoga system. The classes consist of a fixed sequence of 26 poses practiced in a room heated to 105°F (41°C) with a humidity of 40%, designed to reproduce the climate of India.

nidra

Yoga nidra or yogic sleep in modern usage is a state of consciousness between wakefulness and sleep, usually caused by guided meditation. Vegetative symptoms of high anxiety, such as headache, dizziness, chest pain, palpitations, excessive sweating, abdominal pain are well eliminated.

Sensei

Sensei (Japanese: 先生, lit. "born earlier", "prematurely born", "senior") — in Japan, a polite address to a teacher, doctor, writer, boss or other significant person or a much older person. A similar context is carried by the same word (cor. 선생님, sonsennym) in Korea. The same word in China ("xiansheng") is the official polite address "mister".
